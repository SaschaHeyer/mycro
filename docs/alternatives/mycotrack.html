What MycoTrack is genuinely good at

We're not here to talk you out of MycoTrack — of the web-based options it's the closest thing to Mycro, and for a grower who wants to lean into analytics it may be exactly right. Credit where it's due:

  • Deep yield & contamination analytics. On the paid tiers MycoTrack goes further than a simple log — full yield analytics, contamination analytics and environmental logging across your batches.
  • AI-powered insights. Its standout feature is predictive contamination alerts and best-combination (strain × substrate) recommendations — a genuinely interesting layer if you want the software to suggest what to run next.
  • Strain & substrate library. Build a reusable library and track batches spawn → harvest against it, which is handy once you're running many combinations.
  • Web-based, like Mycro. It runs in the browser on any device, so there's nothing to install.

If you want richer analytics and AI recommendations in the browser and you're happy to create an account and pay as you scale, MycoTrack is an excellent pick — you can stop reading here with our blessing.
